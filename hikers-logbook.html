Project Overview

At CareerFoundry, I was tasked with designing a native mobile app for both Android and iOS platforms. This was an open project, so I decided to do a redesign of a little known app, “The Hiker's Logbook”.

Context

The original version of the app is unappealing both visually and functionally. Its core purpose is solid, but needs to grow to stand out in a crowded market. Hikers love to journal their adventures and want more intuitive platforms to relive their treks.

My Role

Goal:

The overall goal of this redesign was to attract more users by making it more appealing to all hikers, not just thru-hikers. Growth and becoming more competitive in the long-term was the focus. To achieve this, there were problems I needed to find solutions for.

Problems:

  • Existing UI lacking visual appeal
  • Target audience too narrowed
  • Lacking expanded features that will appeal to a wider audience.

Solutions:

  • Improve the UI design
  • Implement more interactive journaling features for added user interaction
  • Implement related app functions as this will assure users aren't overwhelmed and the app purpose won't get lost

My Tasks:

  • Product Research
  • User Flows
  • Wireframing
  • Prototyping
  • User Testing
  • UI Design

My Process

Product Research

The first step was understand the direction of the app redesign. This involved researching similar apps on the market, their design patterns, behavior and features.

All Trails

  1. Great design patterns across the app
  2. Easy to follow navigation
  3. Recording feature is intuitive and simple
  4. Accessibility and every element has a place or purpose
  5. Continuity between iOS and Android platforms

Ramblr

  1. This is apps interface feels cluttered
  2. They implemented too many features
  3. The primary purpose of the app seems lost
  4. Liked the video and sound recording features
  5. Liked the log entry location pins on map

User Flow

The user flow chart is rarely final on the first draft for me as it takes fine tuning throughout the UX and UI design process. This insures that the user experience is always the priority for a seamless experience.

Wireframes

Originally built on findings of the product research and my user flows, these laid the ground work for the final structure of the app. Often times I come up with better solutions later in the process, so revisions on these are quite common.

User Testing

I tested a series of prototypes of high fidelity wireframes I created. This is where the solutions I was presenting was validated or showed need for improvement. Valuable information was collected to refine my designs until a final prototype of the end result could be produced.

Test Takeaways

  • There were missed screen and component states in test, confusing testers. /li>
  • I needed to up my Figma prototyping skills to efficiently deliver a test friendly prototype
  • There was a feeling that there were un-needed gestures and elements on screen as this caused confusion during steps. Thus creating a need for more simplicity. I learned less is more here.
  • The added features were validated as it appeared to spark interest with testers
  • The UI design up to this point was strong and added appeal for users

UI Design

I started the process by looking for inspiration. I studied everything outdoor and hiking related. I went with the earth tones you'd expect and a vibrant green shade to give the app some pop. I was sure that the primary and active states had the bright green to gain attention from the users. The guidelines for each platform were carefully incorporated, but at the same time I aimed for continuity between the two version.

Final Prototype

Testers were frustrated that they couldn't start the hike at any given time. They also felt that too much was provided to them outside the primary task. My solution intended for the users to fill out the form before starting the hike. My solution to this was disabling the map and recording controls until the form task was complete. This resulted in the users focus being narrowed down to completing the form first. This solution provided a more streamlined flow that ended with more success. I applied the same approach app wide. Attached is the Android version of the prototype.

Conclusion

I've learned so much about the prototyping during the project. Going forward I will think about the different states I have to design for when I'm working on my initial mockups. Having these states fully designed for in this stage will also prevent the time crunch I felt this time around. I've also learned valuable lessons on how to prototype effectively in Figma, by utilizing more component and layer animations.
