Hiker's Logbook

Hiker's Logbook is a journaling app for hikers that helps them detail and store their experiences in one place, motivating them to explore more and relive their adventures.

App Redesign, Android & iOS Apps

Figma, InVision, Illustrator, Photoshop

UI/UX Designer

6 Weeks

Purpose

The original version of the app is unappealing both visually and functionally. Its core purpose is solid, but needs to appeal to a wider audience of hikers to stand out in a crowded market. The original app focuses solely on thru-hiking stats. Hikers of all kind love to journal their adventures and want more intuitive platforms to relive their treks.

Competitive Analysis

All Trails Analysis:
  • Great design patterns across the app
  • Easy to follow navigation
  • Recording feature is intuitive and simple
  • Accessibility and every element has a place and purpose
  • Continuity between iOS and Android platforms
Ramblr Analysis:
  • The apps interface is cluttered
  • Too many features implemented
  • The array of features aren't cohesive, making the purpose of the app feel lost.
  • Video and sound recording is a unique feature of this kind of app
  • The apps use of location pins for each entry is something I'd like to design for my app
Original App Analysis:
Problems:
  • Existing UI is dull
  • Target audience is too narrowed
  • Lacking features that gives users a true journaling experience.

Solutions:

  • Design a more intuitive UI
  • Offer interactive journaling features that target a wider audience of hikers.
  • Offer functions that compliment each other so the purpose of the app isn't lost.

Define Solutions

This is where I began conceptualizing my solutions by utilizing quick brainstorming and sketching techniques. This included mapping out a user flow for how a user would go about journaling their hike, and wire framing the UI structure of the app.

Design Solutions

This is where I added the visual design to the wireframes I created to begin the finalization of my solutions. I started this stage by looking for inspiration. I studied everything outdoor and hiking related. I went with the earth tones you'd expect and a vibrant green shade to give the app some pop. I was sure that the primary and active states had the bright green to gain attention from the users. The guidelines for each platform were carefully incorporated, but at the same time I aimed for continuity between the two version.

UI Design

Test Solutions

This is where I tested prototypes of my designs and where the solutions I was presenting was validated or showed need for improvement. Valuable information was collected to refine my designs until a final prototype of the end result could be produced.

User Testing Takeaways:
  • There were missed screen and component states in test, confusing testers.
  • I needed to up my Figma prototyping skills to efficiently deliver a test friendly prototype.
  • There was a feeling that there were un-needed gestures and elements on screen as this caused confusion during steps. Less is more was the takeaway here.
  • The added features were validated as it appeared to spark interest with testers.
  • The UI design up to this point was strong and added appeal for users

Revise Solutions

Thru testing, I learned that my first round of designs offered too many elements that confused users during their task. I also learned that simplicity will also help during the prototyping process. Less is more was the main takeaway. Providing only the absolute necessary elements and providing one way to proceed to the next steps is most often the best practice.

Conclusion

I learned that cohesion and simplicity are very important when designing mobile apps. Less is truly more and I was reminded of that throughout the design process. Users download apps for specific reasons and keeping the designs centered on these purposes offer users a higher rate of success and an overall better experience.

Designing with that mindset will also make my process a lot easier. With fewer elements and steps in the designs, the less work there ultimately is, and that carries over to less work for the users as well.
