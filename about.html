My Design Approach

I design with the final product and users in mind. You'll see the same steps of the process being followed in my portfolio as you'll see in every other designers'. What may be refreshing with my process is that my passion for the users and the final product is reflected through my embrace of the revisions along the way.

Having the final product and users in mind is what makes a good design, great! Taking the time to research, collaborate and gather feedback is the evidence of due diligence. Without it, designers are ignoring what may be wrong with their solutions. Furthermore it shows arrogance in that they know more than the user, or worse, they don't care.

I embrace collaboration and feedback that points out where I'm wrong. I'm always looking how the design can be better. Therefore the process I present may not always be clean and cohesive. What may seem like random changes or deviations from original solutions are really the evidence of my brain always pursuing more for the goal of the project; (Effectiveness of the product and user needs).

Experience

For 10+ years I was a production artist in the print industry. My role was to transform designs into finished products. Through this role I learned how design and production go hand in hand. Designs must be compatible with the production or development process. This experience transfers over to UI/UX very well. I know that the designs I create must work for developers and translate into a user friendly finished product.

In 2009 I graduated from Madison Media Institute with an Associates degree in Digital Media Design and Production. When I was in my first production artist role I took the initiative to redesign the companies website. This opened a door with a whole new focus for me.

In 2015 I completed the Web Designer Certificate Program at Milwaukee Area Technical College where I brushed up on front end development and web specific visual design.

The following years I was still in the print industry. I realized I needed to narrow my focus down further towards a more specified path. User Interface Design stood out to me because it's an exciting and ever changing field that will nourish my creative juices and keep me challenged.

In 2022, I enrolled in the UI Design Program at CareerFoundry. This is where I worked on industry centered projects. I learned about the UI design process. During the program I refined my existing visual design skills and added new tools and processes to my skill set.

Most importantly, my time at CareerFoundry allowed me to build a professional portfolio and my confidence. I grew tremendously and so did my passion for UI/UX.
